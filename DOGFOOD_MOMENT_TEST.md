# Dogfooding Moment Test Plan

**Mission:** Evaluate the Moment Engine MVP in real life for 7 days to determine whether moments create genuine emotional value.

---

## 1. Tracking Parameters

For every moment notification generated and delivered, track the following:

### 1.1 Notification Quality
*   **Delightful:** Surprising, warm, and highly personalized.
*   **Useful:** Reminded of a critical detail, prompted productive action.
*   **Neutral:** Factually accurate but did not elicit an emotional reaction.
*   **Annoying:** Intruded at a bad time, generated spammy/repetitive messages.

### 1.2 Experience Feeling
*   **Remembered:** Felt like Nova naturally recalled a conversation.
*   **Generic:** Felt like a rule-based template or a generic calendar reminder.
*   **Intrusive:** Interrupted thoughts or violated boundaries.

### 1.3 User Reaction
*   **Smiled:** Elicited a positive physical or emotional response.
*   **Replied:** The user actively engaged or chatted in response.
*   **Ignored:** Left un-opened or dismissed.
*   **Disabled:** Prompted the user to toggle off the category or preferences.

### 1.4 Loss Valuation
*   Would the user actively miss this feature if it were removed from Human OS tomorrow? (Yes/No/Unsure)

---

## 2. Core Metrics
*   **Open Rate:** $\frac{\text{Moments Opened}}{\text{Moments Generated}}$
*   **Response Rate:** $\frac{\text{Moments Replied}}{\text{Moments Generated}}$
*   **Disable Rate:** $\frac{\text{Preferences Disabled}}{\text{Users Active}}$

---

## 3. Evaluation Goals

*   **Goal:** Determine whether proactive moments create emotional value.
*   **Success Metric:** At least one moment creates a **genuine emotional reaction** (e.g. user smiles or says "that was surprisingly thoughtful").
*   **Failure Metric:** Moments feel like dry calendar reminders, chores, or pushy spam.
